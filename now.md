# Now

_Last updated: May 7, 2026_

I’m currently focused on building across three overlapping areas: manufacturing, software, and international expansion.

## What I’m working on

### Chuanhui’s international presence

I’m working on how Chuanhui should present itself to global customers, especially in HVAC and hydronic heating markets.

A lot of my current thinking is around:

- how to explain technical products clearly to overseas buyers
- how to structure product pages, landing pages, and SEO content
- how to choose better English terminology for Chinese manufacturing categories
- how to build trust through a more professional international website

The goal is not just “make a website,” but to make Chuanhui easier to understand, easier to trust, and easier to buy from.

### Manufacturing and semiconductor-related research

I’ve also been looking into industrial heating components, including wafer heaters, thermocouples, RTDs, and flexible heaters.

This connects to a broader interest of mine: understanding how advanced manufacturing companies communicate technical capability, product reliability, and engineering depth.

I like digging into these niche industrial categories because the surface looks boring, but the real business value is usually hidden in the details.

### Internal tools and project management systems

On the software side, I’m building and refining tools for project management, issue tracking, and analytics.

Recently I’ve been thinking about dashboards inspired by tools like Linear and Swarmia: not just listing tasks, but helping teams see delivery speed, ownership, bottlenecks, and progress more clearly.

I care about software that actually changes how people work, not dashboards that only look nice.

### AI-native workflows

I’m experimenting with AI agents, sandbox environments, and automation workflows.

The direction I’m interested in is: how can AI become part of real work systems, not just a chat box?

That means exploring things like:

- coding agents
- isolated execution environments
- automatic documentation
- internal productivity tools
- better ways to turn scattered work into structured output

## What I’m learning

Right now I’m learning more about:

- international B2B marketing
- SEO content architecture
- technical writing for manufacturing companies
- project analytics
- AI-assisted software development
- how to turn messy business context into clear systems

A recurring theme for me is translation.

Not just language translation, but translating between worlds:

- factory floor → customer-facing website
- Chinese manufacturing know-how → global market language
- scattered tasks → structured software
- intuition → repeatable process

## What I’m thinking about

I’m thinking a lot about how traditional manufacturing companies can become more global, more digital, and more legible.

Many good companies already have the product capability, but they don’t explain themselves well enough.

That gap is interesting to me.

If a company has strong engineering, stable production, and real experience, then the next challenge is making that strength visible to the right people.
